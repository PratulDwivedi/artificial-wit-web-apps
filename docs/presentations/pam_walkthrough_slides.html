Approval Inbox — approvers see everything waiting on them in one place

Workflow 1 · Procure-to-Pay

The purchase journey

A request travels through five stages. Documents are auto-numbered (PR/00001, PO/00001…) and each stage validates the previous one.

STEP 1
Requisition
User requests items with quantities & estimates
STEP 2
Approval
Routed by amount to the right approvers
STEP 3
Purchase Order
Sent to the chosen vendor with taxes computed
STEP 4
Goods Receipt
Record arrival; accept or reject quantities
STEP 5
Invoice & Pay
3-way match & payment (phase 2)
Smart approval routing

Small purchases can auto-approve; larger amounts follow multi-step chains by role or named approver — including delegation when someone is on leave.

Nothing slips through

You can't receive goods against an unapproved PO, and received + rejected quantities can never exceed what was delivered.

Full traceability

Every document links back: GRN → PO → PR. Auditors can follow any purchase end to end.

Getting started

Step 0 — Set up your masters (one time)

Before the first purchase, spend 10 minutes setting up reference data. All under Procurement in the sidebar.

Vendors — Procurement → Vendors → + New

Name is enough to start; add GSTIN, payment terms and credit limit as you go. Vendor codes are generated automatically (VEN/0001).

Items — Procurement → Items → + New

Your purchase catalog. Mark "Is Asset Item" for things that become trackable assets (laptops, machines). Set UoM and tax code so POs compute totals correctly.

Warehouses — Procurement → Warehouses

Where goods are received. One "Main Store" is enough to begin.

Units & Tax Codes — already seeded

EA, BOX, KG, LTR, MTR, HR and GST 5/12/18% + Exempt come pre-loaded. Edit or add your own any time.

Departments, cost centers and locations are shared company-wide masters — they are used across modules, not just procurement.

Workflow 1 · Step 1

Raise a Purchase Requisition

Procurement → Purchase Requisitions

Fill the details

Title (e.g. "Laptops for new joiners"), priority, department, cost center and the date you need it by.

Add line items

Pick items from the catalog (or type a free-text description for new things), set quantity and estimated unit price. The estimated total rolls up automatically.

Save

The PR gets its number (PR/00001) and stays in draft — you can keep editing until you submit.

Submit for approval

Click the green Submit ✓ action on the list row. The system picks the right approval chain based on the amount. If no approval is configured for that range, it approves instantly.

draftin_approval approvedrejected returnedordered

Returned = sent back for rework: fix and re-submit. Only draft/returned PRs can be edited or deleted, and only by their creator (or an admin).

Workflow 1 · Step 2

Approve, reject or return

Procurement → Approval Inbox — one screen for everything waiting on you.

Open your inbox

You only see documents where you are the current approver — by name, by role, or as someone's delegate while they're away.

Review the document

Each row shows the document number, amount, current approval step and who initiated it.

Act with one click

✓ Approve moves it to the next step (or completes it). ✗ Reject stops it. ← Return sends it back to the requester for changes.

How routing works (configurable per company)

Approval chains are defined per document type with amount ranges — e.g. up to ₹50,000: department head only; above that: department head → finance → director. Steps can target a role or a named person, support parallel approvers, escalation timers, and out-of-office delegation. The same engine powers PRs, POs, asset transfers, disposals and invoices.

Workflow 1 · Step 3

Create the Purchase Order

Procurement → Purchase Orders

Pick the vendor

Choose from your vendor master (blacklisted vendors don't appear). Set PO date and expected delivery.

Add the lines

Item, quantity, unit price, discount % and tax code per line.

Totals compute themselves

Line total = qty × price − discount + tax (from the tax code's rate). Header subtotal, discount, tax and grand total roll up automatically — no calculator needed.

Save & track

PO/00001 is created in draft. Its status then follows the real world: approved → sent_to_vendor → partially_received → received.

POs can reference the approved PR they fulfil, keeping the request→order link intact for audit.

Workflow 1 · Step 4

Receive the goods

Procurement → Goods Receipts — when the delivery truck arrives.

Select the PO

The dropdown only shows POs that are actually receivable (approved / sent / partially received). Vendor fills in automatically.

Record the delivery

Receipt date, warehouse, the vendor's challan number and vehicle — everything the gate register needs.

Enter quantities per line

Received, accepted, rejected (with reason). The system blocks impossible entries — accepted + rejected can never exceed received.

GRN/00001 is created

Prices are pulled from the PO lines automatically, so goods-in value is always consistent with what was ordered.

Serial numbers for assets

Receipt lines can carry serial numbers — the foundation for auto-creating asset records from deliveries of asset items (upcoming enhancement).

Workflow 2 · Steps 1–2

Register and allocate assets

Asset Management → Asset Register / Asset Allocation

Register an asset
Asset Register → New

Name, category and location are required; the asset tag auto-generates if you leave it blank.

Add what you know

Serial number, vendor, purchase price and date, warranty expiry, depreciation method. All editable later.

Save

The asset appears in the register as in_stock, ready to allocate.

Allocate & take back
Asset Allocation page

Pick the asset and the employee, optionally an expected return date. Save — custody is recorded and the asset shows allocated with the holder's name.

Deallocate in one click

On the register list, the Deallocate action returns the asset to stock — with the movement logged.

History is automatic

Every allocate / return / transfer writes a movement record: from whom, to whom, when, in what condition.

Workflow 2 · Maintenance

Maintenance work orders

Asset Management → Work Orders — breakdowns and preventive service alike.

Create the work order

Pick the asset, describe the problem, set type (corrective / preventive / calibration…), priority, and assign a technician or an outsourced vendor. WO/00001 is issued.

Build the checklist

Add task rows — "Check oil level", "Replace filter" — mark the mandatory ones. Technicians tick them off with readings and remarks.

Track costs

Labor, vendor and other costs roll up into a total per work order — so you know exactly what each asset costs to keep alive.

Close the loop

Status flows open → in_progress → completed → verified/closed. Downtime hours are captured for reliability reporting.

Preventive maintenance plans (monthly/quarterly schedules that auto-generate work orders) are built into the data model — scheduling automation is on the roadmap.

Trust & Control

Built-in governance

The controls your auditors and finance team will ask about — on by default.

Gap-free numbering — PR/PO/GRN/asset/WO numbers come from locked sequences; no duplicates, no gaps, optionally per fiscal year.
Status guards — submitted documents can't be edited; allocated assets can't be deleted; referenced masters can't be removed.
Ownership rules — you can only delete your own draft requisitions; asset deletion is admin-only.
Complete audit trail — every record stamps who created and last changed it; every approval action and asset movement is logged permanently.
Company isolation — multi-tenant security at the database level; one company can never see another's data.
Record-level access — private / protected / public visibility per record, with role-based access on protected items.
